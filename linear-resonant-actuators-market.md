<p><p>Linear Resonant Actuators (LRAs) are categorized into two main market types: motor type and non-motor type. Motor type LRAs utilize electromagnetic principles to produce linear motion, commonly found in applications requiring precise control and high responsiveness, such as haptic feedback in smartphones. In contrast, non-motor type LRAs typically rely on piezoelectric materials, generating motion through mechanical vibrations. These are often employed in applications requiring compact design and efficient energy usage, like wearables and portable devices.</p></p>
