<p><p>Pulse-tube cryocoolers are categorized into single-stage and two-stage types, each designed for specific cooling applications. Single-stage cryocoolers are typically used for achieving temperatures around 80 K, suitable for applications like infrared detectors and certain superconductors. In contrast, two-stage cryocoolers provide lower temperatures, reaching down to 4 K, making them ideal for applications such as cooling semiconductor devices and enabling advanced scientific research. The choice between these types depends on the required temperature range and application needs.</p></p>
