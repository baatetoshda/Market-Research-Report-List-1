<p><p>Respiratory humidification devices are essential in managing airway moisture during ventilation. The market is categorized into three main types: Invasive Ventilation, which involves endotracheal intubation to deliver humidified air directly to the lungs; Non-Invasive Ventilation, using masks or nasal devices to assist breathing without intubation; and High-Flow Nasal Cannula (HFNC), which provides heated, humidified oxygen through nasal prongs. Each type plays a critical role in enhancing patient comfort and improving respiratory outcomes in various clinical settings.</p></p>
