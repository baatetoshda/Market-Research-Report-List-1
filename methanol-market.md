<p><p>The methanol market primarily includes two significant production methods: the ICI Low Pressure Method and the Lurgi Low Pressure Method. The ICI method involves converting natural gas into synthesis gas at lower pressures, offering cost efficiency and environmental benefits. Conversely, the Lurgi method utilizes a similar process but with different catalysts and reactors, optimizing for larger production scales. Both methods cater to the growing demand for methanol as a versatile chemical feedstock and fuel, influencing pricing and market dynamics.</p></p>
