<p><p>Noncontact temperature measurement encompasses various technologies, primarily infrared and optical fiber methods. Infrared temperature measurement detects thermal radiation emitted from objects to estimate their temperatures without physical contact, making it ideal for moving or hot surfaces. Optical fiber temperature measurement utilizes the principles of light transmission through fiber optics, which changes with temperature variations, allowing precise temperature readings in harsh environments. Both methods enhance safety, efficiency, and accuracy across applications in industries like manufacturing, healthcare, and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
