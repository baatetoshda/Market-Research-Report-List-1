<p><p>The insomnia medication market comprises several types of drugs targeting sleep disorders. Nonbenzodiazepine receptor agonists, such as zolpidem, offer quick sleep onset with fewer side effects than traditional benzodiazepines. Benzodiazepine receptor agonists, including diazepam, provide effective sedation but carry higher risks of dependence. The selective melatonin receptor agonist ramelteon regulates sleep-wake cycles, promoting natural sleep. Sedating antidepressants, such as trazodone, are used off-label for insomnia and address both sleep and mood disorders, broadening treatment options.</p></p>
