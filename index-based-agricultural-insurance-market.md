<p><p>Index-based agricultural insurance is categorized into various market types, primarily including weather index and yield index. The weather index links payouts to specific climatic conditions, such as rainfall levels or temperature extremes, reducing basis risk for farmers. Yield index, on the other hand, ties compensation to crop yield estimates for a particular area, ensuring farmers receive support based on their production losses. Other markets may include price index insurance, which addresses price fluctuations, providing a safety net against market volatility.</p></p>
