<p><p>Cardiac output monitoring equipment is categorized into three types: invasive, minimally invasive, and non-invasive. Invasive methods involve catheterization, providing direct measurements of cardiac output but carrying higher risks. Minimally invasive techniques use fewer instruments to monitor heart performance, balancing accuracy and risk. Non-invasive methods rely on external sensors and diagnostic tools, offering safer, albeit less precise, readings. Each category serves distinct clinical needs, facilitating tailored approaches in managing cardiovascular health and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
