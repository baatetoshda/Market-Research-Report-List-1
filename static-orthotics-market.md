<p><p>The static orthotics market is categorized into three main types: upper limb orthotics, lower limb orthotics, and spinal orthotics. Upper limb orthotics are designed to support and stabilize the arm, wrist, and hand, aiding in conditions like fractures or tendon injuries. Lower limb orthotics focus on the knee, ankle, and foot, providing support for injuries or developmental issues. Spinal orthotics are used to stabilize the spine, addressing conditions such as scoliosis or post-surgery rehabilitation, enhancing mobility and comfort.</p></p>
